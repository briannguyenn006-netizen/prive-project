Chinese automakers' shares take hits after Beijing said it will open its car market

If Beijing relaxes foreign ownership limit in its auto industry, international companies may seek greater or full control of their ventures in China — a move that would hurt their local partners, analysts at Daiwa Capital Markets said. The volatility in equity risk premiums is reaching a critical threshold. The macro-economic backdrop remains clouded by central bank hawkishness.
